1 2 3 4
1
One tenant key

Every table carries org_id. It's the multi-tenant boundary — every query is filtered by it before anything else.

2
Campus hosts many profiles

Institutions sit under a campus (campus_id) but keep org_id too, so one campus can run Primary and Secondary side by side.

3
The sacred FK

Grades, timetables & course registration point at institution_id — so grading rules & terms can differ per level without leaking across tiers.

4
Cross-cutting staff

The junction lets one teacher hold different roles in different (campus, institution) pairs — each is just another assignment row.

1
Pick measure + dimensions

Measure is the number; dimensions are the GROUP BY. Drop the Level dimension and the table collapses to campus totals.

2
Roll-up, not flat

Sub-totals per campus, a grand total, and the underlying query update live — the engine sums leaf institutions up each tier.

3
Tenant-safe by construction

Because every row carries org_id, the same query is safe for a 1-school tenant or a 40-campus group — the filter just widens.

4
Academic ↔ finance join

Headcount comes from institution-scoped academic data; revenue & payroll from campus ledgers — joined on the shared hierarchy keys.
